# Community Experience Skill

Goal: Create a sense of belonging and urgency to join. Move away from feature-selling and move toward people-connection.

## Local Identity (Quito)
- References: Use subtle nods to Quito's tech scene (e.g., mention of local tech hubs, altitude, or "la mitad del mundo" metaphors if they fit the vibe).
- Visuals: If using imagery, prioritize photos of people coding together or recognizable local textures over generic stock office photos.

## The "Tribal" Hierarchy
1. Mission: Why does Cursor Quito exist? (e.g., "The future of AI coding, from the heart of the Andes").
2. Faces/Social Proof: Show that real people are already there.
3. The "Front Door": One primary way to join (Discord, WhatsApp, or Newsletter).
4. Events: Next meetup or active project.

## Hard Rules
- No "Enterprise" jargon. Use "Dev-to-Dev" language.
- Every CTA must lead to a community touchpoint, not a sales demo.
- Include a "How to contribute" or "Open Source" section if applicable.
